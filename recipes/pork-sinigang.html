Pork Sinigang

Pork Sinigang is a classic Filipino soup known for its bold and sour taste. It features tender chunks of pork, vegetables like kangkong (water spinach), and a tamarind-based broth that gives it a distinctive and tangy flavor profile.

Ingredients

  • 500g pork belly (cut into chunks)
  • 500g American pork ribs (cut into individual ribs)
  • 2 medium onions (sliced)
  • 2.5cm knob ginger (smashed and roughly chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (cut into quarters)
  • 150g snake beans (cut into 5cm lengths)
  • 3 chillies (green or red)
  • 1 small eggplant (cut into 5cm lengths)
  • 6-8 okra
  • 200g kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2-2.5 litres water (depending on preferred sour level)
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 pkt Sinigang tamarind soup mix
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt to taste (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat up a casserole pot with oil and saut√© onions until translucent.
  2. Toss in the pork belly and pork ribs and cook through for about 10 mins.
  3. Add in the smashed ginger and stir for 1 min, then toss in the tomatoes. Then, add in the fish sauce and stir through.
  4. Pour in some water and bring it to a boil, then once boiling, lower heat and simmer for 40 mins under low fire.
  5. Add in the tamarind soup mix, chillies, snake beans and eggplant and let it boil for 8 mins.
  6. Toss in the okra and simmer for 3 mins.
  7. Add in the kangkong, then switch off fire and allow the residual heat to cook the kangkong.
  8. Serve while hot.
